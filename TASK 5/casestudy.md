UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a prevalent form of cybercrime in India where scammers manipulate users to steal money through unauthorized bank transactions. The crime typically exploits social engineering rather than hacking the robust UPI infrastructure itself. It often happens when a scammer poses as a buyer on an online marketplace (like OLX) or a bank representative, contacting the victim via phone. Step-by-step, the fraudster convinces the victim to click a "collect request" link or scan a QR code, promising to "receive" payment, but actually triggering a payment from the victim. Anyone using digital payment apps is targeted, but fraudsters often focus on senior citizens, first-time UPI users, or sellers on classified sites who are eager to close a sale. 

The consequences of such fraud are significant, often resulting in immediate, unapproved financial losses, which can exceed lakhs of rupees in organized scams. While RBI guidelines provide mechanisms for reporting, recovering funds is challenging if the user voluntarily shares their UPI PIN or OTP. Victims face not only monetary loss but also severe emotional distress, time-consuming complaints, and potential "money mule" involvement. Ultimately, these scams erode trust in digital payment systems and require strict vigilance, such as never entering a PIN to receive money.

To stay safe, users must follow the golden rule: A UPI PIN is required only to send money, never to receive it. Avoid sharing OTPs, clicking on suspicious links, or scanning unknown QR codes. If a fraud occurs, immediately call the national helpline 1930 or report it on cybercrime.gov.in. Education is the best defense; awareness is key to ensuring that the digital payments "fairytale" remains safe and secure for all.
