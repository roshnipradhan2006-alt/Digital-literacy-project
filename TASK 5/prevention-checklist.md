
# General Online Security Tips : 
1-  Use Strong, Unique Passwords: Use a password manager to create and store complex passwords (letters, numbers, symbols) to avoid reusing them across accounts.
2-  Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA/2FA): Add a second layer of security (like an OTP or app code) to all critical accounts.
3-  Update Software Regularly: Keep your operating system, browsers, and antivirus software updated to patch vulnerabilities.
4-  Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Transactions: Do not conduct financial transactions on public Wi-Fi; use a Virtual Private Network (VPN) if necessary.
5-  Beware of Phishing: Do not click on unknown links or download attachments in suspicious emails or messages. 


# UPI & Financial Safety Tips : 
1-  Never Share PIN/OTP: UPI PINs are for sending money, not receiving it. Never share PINs or OTPs over the phone.
2-  Verify Recipient Details: Always check the name on the screen before entering your UPI PIN.
3-  Be Wary of "Urgent" Requests: Scammers often create a false sense of urgency. Slow down and verify before making payments.
4-  Set Transaction Limits: Lower the limits on UPI apps and credit/debit cards to minimum required levels.
5-  Check Bank Statements Regularly: Review transactions weekly to detect unauthorized activity. 


# National Cybercrime Prevention Tips :
1-  Report Cyber Crime Promptly: Immediately report any fraudulent activity by calling the national helpline 1930 or filing a complaint on cybercrime.gov.in.
2-  Secure Personal Documents: Be cautious about providing PAN, Aadhaar, or address proof to unknown persons, as this can lead to SIM cloning.
3-  Do Not Install Unknown Apps: Only download apps from official app stores to avoid malware and screen-sharing frauds.
4-  Check Privacy Settings: Regularly review social media privacy settings to restrict who can see your personal information.
5-  Use Anti-Malware Software: Install reputable antivirus software on your phone and computer.
